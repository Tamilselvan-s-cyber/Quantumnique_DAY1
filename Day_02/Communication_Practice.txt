BLOCK 3 – COMMUNICATION PRACTICE


Explain Your Final Year Project

Project Title: Online Voting System


Problem Statement:

                Traditional paper-based voting is slow, prone to human error, and difficult to manage. Many 
                eligible voters are unable to participate due to geographical barriers or physical disabilities,
                leading to lower engagement and potential security risks in manual counting.


Technologies Used :


               Frontend: HTML, CSS (Tailwind for responsive design), and JavaScript (React.js ).
               Backend : Node.js with Express.js, PHP to handle server-side logic.
               Database:  MongoDB (NoSQL) for flexible data handling.


Your Role in the Project:


              (1) Full-Stack Developer: Responsible for the end-to-end development, including designing the voter UI, 
                   building the secure backend API, and integrating the database.

              (2) Security Engineer: Focused on implementing encryption protocols, preventing SQL injection,
                  and ensuring the "one-voter-one-vote" logic was unhackable.

              (3) Database Administrator (DBA): Designed the schema to handle thousands of concurrent entries 
                   and optimized queries for real-time result tallying. 


Challenges Faced:
              
         Online voting faces critical cybersecurity risks like malware and DDoS attacks that can compromise ballot
         integrity or block access.The system must also solve the secrecy-verifiability paradox, ensuring votes remain 
         anonymous while allowing voters to confirm their choice was counted.Additionally, the lack of a physical audit 
         trail makes manual recounts impossible, often leading to low public trust and legal hurdles.
